# JRHOF Launch Vision

## End goal

The end goal is neither a modern redesign nor a perfect clone of WordPress. It is a production-familiar, trusted JRHOF website running on Astro and Cloudflare: recognizable to the existing community, easier to use, safer to operate, and maintainable by the people responsible for it.

Production familiarity is valuable where it creates trust. Preserve the recognizable JRHOF/CHSBUA identity, homepage rhythm, archive character, event storytelling, visual restraint, and direct nonprofit tone.

Astro improvements are valuable where production is broken, risky, stale, inaccessible, confusing, or operationally weak. Keep the complete 150-person archive, reliable links, reconciled source handling, responsive behavior, semantic/accessibility improvements, static security posture, and removal of WordPress account, comment, sharing, and plugin clutter.

## Environment note

The GitHub repository is connected to Cloudflare Workers, and the current development URL is [jrhof-webapp.tmco-consulting.workers.dev](https://jrhof-webapp.tmco-consulting.workers.dev). Treat it as a development environment only. Production remains [jrhof.org](https://jrhof.org/) until formal cutover.

## Product principles

- Familiar does not mean copying known defects.
- Modern does not mean adopting startup, SaaS, or campaign-landing-page conventions.
- Cleaner implementation should make the organization feel more trustworthy, not less recognizable.
- Public pages should communicate finished, visitor-centered information; migration and editorial workflow notes stay internal.
- Unapproved facts or workflows should be withheld or presented neutrally, never simulated with active-looking controls.
- Every new feature needs an operational owner and an approved end-to-end policy, not only a front-end treatment.

## Future target state

The mature JRHOF platform should support:

- Native donations with server-verified payments, approved tax/receipt language, acknowledgments, refunds, reporting, and support ownership.
- Approved sponsorship packages with clear fulfillment, inventory, payment or inquiry flow, and accountable ownership.
- Native banquet registration with capacity, attendee data, payment state, confirmations, changes/cancellations, and event operations.
- Native golf registration with teams, add-ons, capacity, payment state, confirmations, and event operations.
- Strong technical and editorial SEO, including canonical URLs, redirects, metadata, sitemap coverage, appropriate structured data, and durable content quality.
- Google Ad Grants readiness, including policy-compliant landing pages, consent-aware measurement, meaningful conversions, and no stale or fragmented visitor journeys.

These capabilities are the destination, not authorization to implement them now. Each requires approved requirements, security/privacy review, operational ownership, and its own acceptance criteria.

## Launch test

The site is ready when an existing JRHOF visitor finds it familiar and credible, a new visitor can understand and act without confusion, the archive is accurate and complete, and the organization can safely operate every published workflow after launch.
